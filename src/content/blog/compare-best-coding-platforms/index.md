| **Pricing** | $10-$200 / course | $59/mo | $29/mo |
| **Teachers** | Independent Creators | University Professors | Industry Experts |
| **Best For** | Specific practical skills | Theory & Degrees | Enterprise Teams |

## 1. Udemy

**The Marketplace for Everything.**
Udemy is like the YouTube of education but with higher quality control. It's the best place to find niche, up-to-date tutorials on the absolute latest tech (e.g., a new JS framework released last week).

*   **Pros:** Massive library, pay once keep forever, very practical.
*   **Cons:** Quality varies wildly between instructors.

## 2. Coursera

**The Academic Hall.**
Coursera partners with Yale, Stanford, and Google. If you want a credential that looks good on a LinkedIn profile or are chasing a formal degree, this is the spot.

*   **Pros:** Recognized certificates, academic rigor, financial aid available.
*   **Cons:** Can be dry/theoretical, deadlines, monthly subscription.

## 3. Pluralsight

**The Tech Skill Graph.**
Focused purely on IT and dev skills. It assesses your skill level and recommends a learning path.

*   **Pros:** High production value, skill assessments, no "fluff" courses.
*   **Cons:** Expensive monthly cost, not great for non-technical topics.

## Verdict

*   **Choose [Udemy](https://trk.udemy.com/c/6564357/3193860/39854)** if you want to build a project using a specific tool *this weekend*.
*   **Choose [Coursera](https://www.coursera.org)** if you want a career change and need a recognized certificate.
*   **Choose [Pluralsight](https://www.pluralsight.com)** if your employer is paying for it to upskill your team.
