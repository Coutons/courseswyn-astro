1. Use Coupon Sites Like CoursesWyn (Best for Daily Free Courses)

The fastest and most reliable way to get paid Udemy courses for free is through 100% OFF coupon codes. Instructors release these coupons to attract initial students and build reviews for new courses.

How it works:

  • Visit CoursesWyn.com daily — it aggregates fresh 100% off coupons every 24 hours.
  • Browse by category (Development, IT, Business, Design, etc.).
  • Click a course you want. If the coupon is still active, Udemy will show the price as $0.00.

2. Use Your Public Library Card (Hidden Gem)

This is the most underrated method on the entire internet — and it’s completely free, no credit card required.

Udemy has partnered with Gale, an educational technology company, to offer free course access through thousands of public libraries and universities. If your library participates, you can access a massive collection of Udemy courses just by signing in with your library card.

How to check and use it:

  1. Go to your local public library’s website and search for “Udemy” or “Gale Courses.”
  2. Log in with your library card number.
  3. Access thousands of courses for free — no subscription, no credit card.

Works in: Many US public libraries, some Canadian and UK institutions, and select universities globally.

💡 Pro Tip: Even if your home library doesn’t participate, you can often get a free digital card from a participating library in another city (many offer this online). It’s worth a 5-minute check.

3. Start a Udemy Personal Plan Free Trial (7 Days, 26,000+ Courses)

Udemy offers a 7-day free trial for new subscribers to its Personal Plan. During those 7 days, you get full access to over 26,000 top-rated courses covering web development, data science, IT certification, digital marketing, AI, and more.

How to claim the free trial:

  1. Go to udemy.com/personal-plan.
  2. Click “Start free trial.”
  3. Enter your payment details (required, but you won’t be charged if you cancel before day 7).
  4. Learn as much as possible — then cancel before the trial ends.

What’s included in the trial:

  • Unlimited access to 26,000+ curated courses
  • AI-powered coding exercises and practice tests
  • Goal-focused learning recommendations
  • Access on desktop, mobile, and tablet

⚠️ Important: Set a calendar reminder to cancel before Day 7. The trial auto-converts to a paid subscription (~$20/month) if not canceled.

4. Filter by “Free” on Udemy Directly

Udemy hosts thousands of permanently free courses. These are usually shorter introductory courses designed to give you a taste of a topic or an instructor’s teaching style — but many are genuinely valuable.

Steps to find free courses:

  1. Go to udemy.com and search for any topic (e.g., “Python”, “Excel”, “UI/UX”).
  2. In the left-hand filter sidebar, scroll to “Price”.
  3. Check “Free”.
  4. Sort by Highest Rated to find the best quality free options.

Best free topics available on Udemy right now:

  • Python basics
  • HTML & CSS fundamentals
  • Intro to Machine Learning
  • Digital Marketing basics
  • Excel for beginners
  • Ethical hacking introduction

5. Follow Instructors on Social Media for Exclusive Promo Links

Top Udemy instructors often share exclusive free enrollment links directly with their social media followers — before posting them to any coupon site. This gives their community first access.

Where to follow:

  • YouTube – Many instructors post free course links in video descriptions or community posts.
  • LinkedIn – Professional instructors (especially in business and IT) frequently share limited promo codes.
  • Twitter/X – Quick announcements for flash free-access windows.
  • Facebook Groups – Many instructors run official student groups where they drop free coupons.

How to find instructors to follow:

  1. Enroll in a free course or any course you already own.
  2. Go to the instructor’s profile page on Udemy.
  3. Find their social media links listed in their bio.
  4. Follow and turn on notifications.

💡 Pro Tip: Instructors who are launching a new course often give 100% off access for the first 48–72 hours to boost initial enrollment numbers. Being an early follower gives you a major advantage.

6. Join Beta Testing Opportunities

When instructors are building a new course, they sometimes recruit beta testers to review content and give feedback in exchange for free lifetime access.

Where to find beta testing opportunities:

  • Reddit: Subreddits like r/learnprogramming, r/webdev, r/digitalnomad, and r/udemy occasionally have posts from instructors seeking testers.
  • Discord Servers: Many developer and design communities have instructors who post beta invitations.
  • Udemy’s Own Community Boards: Check forums related to your topic of interest.
  • Direct Email: If you’ve taken a course from an instructor you like, reply to their Udemy email or contact them through their website to express interest in future beta programs.

Beta access typically comes with the expectation that you’ll leave a detailed, honest review after completing the course.

Pro Tips to Maximize Your Free Learning

Getting a free course is just step one. Here’s how to actually get value from it:

Enroll first, learn later. Once you enroll in a free course, you get lifetime access even after the coupon expires. Grab courses now even if your schedule is busy — you can watch them anytime.

Focus on highly rated courses. Filter for 4.4+ star ratings with at least 1,000 reviews. Free doesn’t mean low quality — many free coupon courses are full-length paid courses temporarily made available.

Supplement with YouTube. For topics like Python, JavaScript, or Excel, combine your free Udemy courses with free YouTube tutorials from the same instructor to reinforce concepts.

Get certificates where possible. Udemy course completion certificates are included even in free (coupon-accessed) courses, and they can be shared on your LinkedIn profile.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are 100% off Udemy coupons legal?

Yes. Instructors themselves create and distribute these coupons through Udemy’s official system. Using them is completely legitimate and within Udemy’s terms of service.

How long does free Udemy enrollment last?

When you enroll in a course using a coupon (even a 100% off coupon), you receive lifetime access to that course — just like any paid purchase.

Do free Udemy courses include a certificate?

Yes. Most Udemy courses — including those accessed via 100% off coupons — include a certificate of completion.

What’s the difference between permanently free courses and coupon courses?

Permanently free courses are always free and tend to be shorter. Coupon courses are paid courses with a temporary 100% off code — they’re usually full-length (10+ hours) and significantly more comprehensive.

Can I access Udemy for free without a credit card?

Yes — through the library card method (no card required) or by enrolling in permanently free courses directly on Udemy.
